Let’s start with a game.

A coin is hidden among four opaque cups. At each round, we ask yes/no questions to uncover the cup containing the coin. Multiple rounds are played and we aim to minimize the number of questions asked.

Mutual information and channel capacity

Given two discrete random variables XXX and YYY we note,
I(X,Y)=H(X)+H(Y)−H(XY)I(X,Y)=H(X)+H(Y)-H(XY)I(X,Y)=H(X)+H(Y)−H(XY)

A communication channel is modeled by a source of input messages XXX transmitted and received as output messages YYY on the other end of the channel. The conditional probability P(Y∣X)P(Y|X)P(Y∣X) defines the reliability of the channel.

Given a fixed conditional distribution P(Y∣X)P(Y|X)P(Y∣X), we can try to modify the distribution of the input messages P(X)P(X)P(X) to maximize the mutual information I(X,Y)I(X,Y)I(X,Y). This maximal mutual information is called the capacity of the channel.
